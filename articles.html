Chill Out for Your Back's Sake

It is easy to do, you can easily find yourself get stressed and crunched for time which no matter how many times you try you cannot become anymore productive. But have you ever thought about just forcing yourself to take a break and relax? Well you should!

A recent study noted that people who were more likely to take a break every 2 hours of at least 10 minutes were less likely to be injured. But why does taking breaks means a more vigorous immune system? Its because the stress hormone cortisol, which is linked to fat build up as well, prioritizes itself for immeadiate threats and lets the immune system bare the brunt of the reallocation overhead.

Use Red Light Filters to Get A Good Sleep

Sleep is an important time for our bodies to catch up with us and is incredibly useful for a variety of physiological processes. Therefore we need to make sure we are doing everything in our power to get as much sleep as we need every night of the week. One reason some people have trouble sleeping is because the brain can't tell when its lights out when you are staring at your phone for hours.

The best way to mitigate this unpleasant rammification of your digital lifestyle is with filters that strip the red light from the screen. More blue light in your eyes than red creates an effect similar to moon and help cue the body that it is time to start shutting down and get ready for the excitement tomorrow will bring!

Diet and Health: An Obvious Pair

While you shouldn't be shocked to hear it, it is worth stating that your health and your diet are intimately tied together. This doesn't mean that by eating "diet" food or carefully counting your calories are the only way to good health, as exercise also builds self-discipline but in a much healthier way than dieting alone.

But What to Eat?
